SHORT BIOGRAPHY

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. His groundbreaking work on the theory of relativity, famously summarized by the equation E=mcÂ², reshaped modern physics. Einstein's ideas had a profound impact on various fields and earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921. His genius and intellectual curiosity continue to inspire scientific exploration to this day.
In addition to his groundbreaking contributions to physics, Albert Einstein was also an outspoken advocate for civil rights and social justice. He used his platform and intellect to address issues of racism, war, and inequality. Born in Ulm, Germany, Einstein later became a Swiss citizen and eventually moved to the United States. He held academic positions at renowned institutions such as the Swiss Patent Office, the University of Zurich, and the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
